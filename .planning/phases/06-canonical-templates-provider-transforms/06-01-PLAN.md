<objective>
Deliver the **semantic core** of the product: pure desired-state transforms that decide what "make Z.ai the default" means in Codex `config.toml` vocabulary.

`apply_zai(doc) -> doc` and `apply_openai(doc) -> doc` are symmetric pure functions over a `tomlkit.TOMLDocument` (no IO; they live in `services/` per D-09). They are **exact inverses** (SC-2: `apply_openai(apply_zai(doc)) == apply_openai(doc)`, Z.ai block preserved on revert) and **idempotent** by construction (re-applying is a no-op). A canonical desired-state source of truth (the Z.ai provider block body + the OpenAI default pointers) exists as module-level constants (SC-1). A pure predicate `check_postconditions(doc)` asserts the transformed doc resolves to a valid provider (SC-3, CONF-05).

Purpose: This is the declarative brain â€” Phase 5's `TomlBackend` is the generic read/write/upsert surface; Phase 6 fills it with meaning (which keys, which values). Phase 7's `use zai`/`use openai` handlers read this doc, call these transforms, write via `TomlBackend`, then call `check_postconditions`.

Output: `src/zai_codex_helper/services/providers.py` (pure domain), `tests/test_providers.py`, updated `services/__init__.py` docstring.
</objective>
